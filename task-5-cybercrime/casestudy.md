# Case Study: UPI/Online Payment Fraud

## Overview

UPI (Unified Payments Interface) fraud is one of the 
fastest growing cybercrimes in India, particularly 
targeting college students and young adults who rely 
heavily on digital payment apps like Google Pay, 
PhonePe, and Paytm for daily transactions.

## How It Typically Happens (Step-by-Step)

1. The attacker identifies a target — usually someone 
   who has posted a "item for sale" listing on OLX, 
   Instagram, or a college WhatsApp group.

2. The fraudster contacts the seller pretending to be 
   an interested buyer, often claiming to be an Army 
   officer or a professional to build trust.

3. They send a fake UPI payment screenshot claiming 
   the money has been transferred and ask the seller 
   to "confirm" by scanning a QR code or entering 
   their UPI PIN.

4. The victim unknowingly authorizes a payment 
   REQUEST instead of receiving money — resulting 
   in money being debited from their own account.

5. The fraudster immediately blocks the victim and 
   disappears.

## Who Is Usually Targeted

College students selling second-hand items, people 
looking for part-time work online, and individuals 
responding to "too good to be true" job or cashback 
offers are the most common targets.

## Consequences

Victims lose money instantly with little chance of 
recovery. Beyond financial loss, victims experience 
mental stress, loss of trust in digital payments, 
and in some cases, identity theft if they shared 
personal documents during the transaction.

## Source

Based on cybercrime patterns reported by the National 
Cyber Crime Reporting Portal (cybercrime.gov.in) and 
RBI guidelines on UPI safety.
