### For College Students in India

## ✅ General Online Safety

- [ ] **Use strong, unique passwords** for every account. A strong password has 12+ characters with a mix of letters, numbers, and symbols. Use a password manager (like Bitwarden, free) rather than reusing the same password everywhere.

- [ ] **Enable Two-Factor Authentication (2FA)** on all important accounts — email, social media, and banking apps. This ensures that even if your password is stolen, your account stays protected.

- [ ] **Never click suspicious links in SMS, WhatsApp, or email** — even if the sender appears to be your bank, college, or a government body. Always navigate directly to the official website by typing the URL yourself.

- [ ] **Verify before you trust** — If you receive a call or message from someone claiming to represent a bank, scholarship body, or government office, hang up and call the official number listed on their verified website.

- [ ] **Keep your devices and apps updated.** Security patches in updates close known vulnerabilities that scammers actively exploit. Enable auto-updates wherever possible.

- [ ] **Use only trusted, secured Wi-Fi networks.** Avoid logging into banking apps or entering passwords on public Wi-Fi (in cafes, hostels, or college canteens). Use a VPN if you must connect to public networks.

---

## 💸 UPI & Financial Safety (Mandatory for Indian Students)

- [ ] **Remember this rule — there is NO QR code or PIN to RECEIVE money.** If anyone asks you to scan a QR code, enter your UPI PIN, or "verify" your account to receive a payment or scholarship — it is a scam. Immediately disconnect and report.

- [ ] **Never share your UPI PIN, OTP, or CVV with anyone** — not with bank staff, not with "customer support" callers, not with friends. Legitimate institutions will never ask for these details over a call or message.

- [ ] **Set a daily UPI transaction limit** in your payment app settings. This caps potential losses if your phone or credentials are ever compromised.

- [ ] **Check the recipient name carefully before confirming any UPI payment.** Scammers create UPI IDs that closely resemble legitimate ones (e.g., `support.hdfc@upi` instead of `hdfcbank@hdfcbank`).

---

## 🔒 Social Media & Identity Safety

- [ ] **Audit your social media privacy settings** every semester. Make sure strangers cannot see your phone number, home address, or daily location. Limit posts to "Friends Only" unless there is a specific reason to make them public.

- [ ] **Do not accept friend requests from unknown profiles** that have few posts, no mutual friends, and were created recently — these are common traits of fake accounts used for scams or social engineering.

## 📣 How to Report Cybercrime in India

| Channel | Details |
|---------|---------|
| 📞 Helpline | **1930** — National Cyber Crime Helpline (24×7, free) |
| 🌐 Online Portal | **[cybercrime.gov.in](https://cybercrime.gov.in)** — File complaints for financial fraud, cyberbullying, identity theft, and more |
| 🏫 College | Report to your college's student grievance cell or IT department for incidents involving college platforms |
| 🚔 Local Police | Visit your nearest cyber cell — most district police stations now have one |

> **Act fast:** In UPI fraud cases, report to your bank's fraud helpline AND file on cybercrime.gov.in within the first hour. Early reporting significantly improves the chance of freezing the transaction.
