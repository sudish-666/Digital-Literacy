# Case Study: UPI/Online Payment Fraud

**Cybercrime Type:** UPI & Online Payment Fraud
**Task 5 – Cybercrime Awareness | Digital Literacy Assignment | VIT Bhopal University**

---

## Overview

UPI (Unified Payments Interface) fraud is one of the fastest-growing cybercrimes in India. It exploits the convenience and speed of digital payments — and the limited awareness of first-time users — to steal money in seconds. College students, who are typically new to managing their own finances and rely heavily on UPI apps like PhonePe, Google Pay, and Paytm, are among the most frequently targeted groups.

---

## Realistic Scenario: "The Scholarship Scam"

Riya, a first-year engineering student at a central university in Madhya Pradesh, received a WhatsApp message from an unknown number. The sender claimed to be from the "National Student Scholarship Disbursement Cell" and told Riya she had been selected for a ₹15,000 merit scholarship. To "verify her account and release the funds," she was asked to scan a QR code sent over WhatsApp.

**Here is how the fraud unfolded, step by step:**

1. **Lure** — Riya was promised a scholarship she hadn't applied for, creating excitement and lowering her guard.
2. **Urgency** — She was told the offer would expire within 30 minutes, preventing careful thought.
3. **QR Code Trick** — The scammer sent a *payment request* QR code (not a receive-money code). Scanning it would debit money *from* Riya's account — not credit it.
4. **Execution** — Riya scanned the code and entered her UPI PIN, believing she was "verifying" her bank account. ₹8,500 was instantly debited.
5. **Disappearance** — The number was blocked immediately after the transaction went through.

---

## Who Is Typically Targeted?

- First-year college students unfamiliar with UPI mechanics
- Students from semi-urban or rural backgrounds newly exposed to digital banking
- Anyone responding to social media posts about "free money," scholarships, or part-time jobs
- Users on OLX/Quikr buying or selling items (fake buyer/seller scams)

---

## Consequences

| Impact | Detail |
|--------|--------|
| **Financial** | Immediate monetary loss; UPI transactions are near-impossible to reverse once completed |
| **Psychological** | Shame, anxiety, and loss of trust in digital platforms |
| **Academic** | Financial stress affecting focus and performance |
| **Legal complexity** | Victims often don't report due to embarrassment, allowing scammers to repeat the crime |

---

## Source / Basis

This scenario is a **realistic composite** based on patterns documented by:
- RBI's "RBI Kehta Hai" consumer awareness campaigns (rbi.org.in)
- NPCI (National Payments Corporation of India) fraud alerts
- Reports published by the Indian Cybercrime Coordination Centre (I4C) under MHA

Real incidents of near-identical QR code scams targeting students have been reported across Madhya Pradesh, Rajasthan, and UP in 2023–2024.

---

## Key Takeaway

> **Scanning a QR code to *receive* money is technically impossible on any UPI app. If someone asks you to scan a code or share your UPI PIN to receive money — it is always a scam.**
