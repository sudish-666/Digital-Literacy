Social Media Do's and Don'ts for College Students
Task 4 – Digital Literacy Assignment | VIT Bhopal University

✅ DO's
# Do
Why It Matters
1. Keep your profiles professional – Use a clear profile photo and a bio that accurately represents you.
Recruiters and internship coordinators frequently review social profiles before shortlisting candidates.
2. Think before you post – Ask yourself: "Would I be comfortable if my professor, parent, or future employer saw this?"
Posts are permanent; even deleted content can be screenshotted and shared.
3. Engage positively and constructively – Comment with thoughtful opinions; support peers' work and achievements.
Positive engagement builds your network and your reputation simultaneously.
4. Use privacy settings wisely – Regularly review who can see your posts, photos, and personal information.
Limits the exposure of personal data to strangers and unwanted audiences.
5. Share your learning journey – Post about certifications, projects, hackathons, and achievements on LinkedIn or GitHub.
Builds a professional digital footprint that supports internship and scholarship applications.
6. Verify information before sharing – Check sources before forwarding news, statistics, or claims.
Sharing misinformation damages credibility and can spread harm.
7. Separate personal and professional spaces – Maintain a LinkedIn profile distinct from casual social accounts.
Helps you tailor your image for different audiences appropriately.


❌ DON'Ts
# Don't
Why It Matters
1. Don't post confidential information – Avoid sharing exam papers, internal college communications, or institutional data.
Violates academic integrity policies and can have serious disciplinary consequences.
2. Don't engage in cyberbullying or hate speech – Never target individuals with harassment, mockery, or discriminatory language.
Causes real psychological harm and can result in legal consequences or college disciplinary action.
3. Don't overshare personal details – Avoid publicly posting your phone number, address, daily schedule, or location.
Creates vulnerability to identity theft, stalking, and social engineering attacks.
4. Don't post under the influence of strong emotions – Avoid venting frustration about professors, classmates, or institutions.
Emotional posts are hard to take back and can permanently damage relationships or opportunities.
5. Don't plagiarise content – Always credit the original creator when sharing artwork, writing, code, or ideas.
Intellectual property theft is both unethical and, in many cases, a legal violation.
6. Don't ignore red flags in DMs – Be cautious of strangers offering opportunities that seem too good to be true.
Online scams targeting students (fake internships, scholarship frauds) are increasingly common.
7. Don't neglect your digital footprint – Avoid leaving old, embarrassing, or contradictory content publicly visible.
Future employers, graduate schools, and scholarship committees conduct online searches.
